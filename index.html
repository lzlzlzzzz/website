About Westlake

Westlake Girls High School, a state girls secondary school, was established in 1958 as a coeducational school and changed to girls only in 1962. The school has a strong focus on all aspects of academics, extracurricular activities and student wellbeing. With more than 30 clubs, more than 30 sports and more than 10 music ensemble groups, each student can find and develop their strengths and be the best they can be.

Westlake Girls was divided into five Houses in 2013, named after significant local areas. The house colours relate to the meaning of the house names and emulate the pursuit of excellence inspired by the Olympic rings. The Houses and their colours are Akoranga (black), Hauraki (gold), Onewa (red), Pupuke (blue) and Wairau (green). Throughout the year the houses compete in a variety of events and select a house charity to support.
